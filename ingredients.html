Ingredient Encyclopedia

Know What's On Your Skin

Every ingredient explained in plain English. No hidden chemicals, no mystery formulas.

Baobab Oil

Hero Ingredient

INCI: Adansonia Digitata Seed Oil

What It Does

Deep moisturization, rich in vitamins A, D, E, and F. Repairs damaged skin barrier and promotes elasticity.

Traditional Use: Used for centuries across Africa for skin healing and hair care.

Source: Kenyan communities, fair trade certified.

Grass-Fed Tallow

Hero Ingredient

INCI: Adeps Bovis

What It Does

Mimics human skin's natural oils. Deeply nourishing, non-comedogenic, and rich in fat-soluble vitamins.

Traditional Use: Ancestral skincare ingredient used before modern cosmetics.

Source: Ethically sourced from Kenyan farms.

Unrefined Shea Butter

Hero Ingredient

INCI: Butyrospermum Parkii Butter

What It Does

Anti-inflammatory, deeply moisturizing. Protects against environmental damage and soothes irritated skin.

Traditional Use: Known as "women's gold" in West Africa for its healing properties.

Source: Women's cooperatives, fair trade premium.

Bentonite Clay

Detox

INCI: Bentonite

What It Does

Natural detoxifier that draws out impurities. Balances oily skin and unclogs pores without harsh chemicals.

Traditional Use: Ancient remedy for skin purification and healing.

Source: Volcanic deposits, naturally occurring mineral.

Virgin Coconut Oil

Moisturizer

INCI: Cocos Nucifera Oil

What It Does

Antimicrobial, deeply hydrating. Creates protective barrier while allowing skin to breathe naturally.

Traditional Use: Coastal communities have used it for millennia for skin and hair.

Source: Cold-pressed from Kenyan coconuts.

Castor Oil

Growth

INCI: Ricinus Communis Seed Oil

What It Does

Promotes hair growth, strengthens lashes and brows. High in ricinoleic acid for anti-inflammatory benefits.

Traditional Use: Used in African and Ayurvedic medicine for centuries.

Source: Cold-pressed, hexane-free extraction.

Skin bleaching chemical banned in many countries. We celebrate natural skin.
